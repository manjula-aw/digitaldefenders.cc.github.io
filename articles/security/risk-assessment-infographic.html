ISO 27005 Risk Assessment Process

1 Context Establishment

Define the scope, boundaries, and criteria for risk assessment

  • Define scope: Which systems, processes, and information assets?
  • Risk criteria: Establish risk appetite and tolerance levels
  • Stakeholders: Identify who needs to be involved
  • Methodology: Choose qualitative or quantitative approach

2 Risk Identification

Identify assets, threats, vulnerabilities, and existing controls

  • Assets: Hardware, software, data, people, facilities
  • Threats: Natural disasters, cyber attacks, human error
  • Vulnerabilities: Weaknesses that can be exploited
  • Existing controls: Current security measures in place

3 Risk Analysis

Assess the likelihood and impact of identified risks

  • Likelihood assessment: How probable is the threat?
  • Impact assessment: What damage could occur?
  • Risk level calculation: Risk = Likelihood √ó Impact
  • Consider: Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability impacts

4 Risk Evaluation

Compare risks against criteria and prioritize treatment

  • Compare: Risk level vs. risk acceptance criteria
  • Prioritize: Rank risks from highest to lowest
  • Decision: Accept, mitigate, transfer, or avoid?
  • Business context: Consider organizational priorities

5 Risk Treatment

Select and implement controls to modify risks

  • Risk modification: Implement security controls
  • Risk retention: Accept the risk with justification
  • Risk avoidance: Eliminate the risk source
  • Risk sharing: Transfer to third party (insurance)

6 Risk Acceptance

Obtain management approval for residual risks

  • Document: All risk treatment decisions
  • Sign-off: Management formally accepts residual risk
  • Communicate: Share decisions with stakeholders
  • Record: Maintain risk register and treatment plan

üîë ISO 27005 Key Principles

  • Continuous process: Risk assessment is iterative and ongoing
  • Aligned with ISO 27001: Supports ISMS implementation
  • Flexible methodology: Adapt to organizational needs
  • Management involvement: Senior leadership must be engaged
  • Documentation: Maintain comprehensive records

OWASP Risk Rating Methodology

1 Identify Risk

Discover the vulnerability and understand the threat

  • Vulnerability identification: Security testing, code review
  • Threat agent: Who might exploit this?
  • Attack vector: How can it be exploited?
  • Context: Application architecture and data flow

2 Estimate Likelihood

Assess threat agent factors and vulnerability factors

Threat Agent Factors:
  • Skill level: How skilled is the attacker?
  • Motive: What's their incentive?
  • Opportunity: How easy is access?
  • Size: How large is the threat group?
Vulnerability Factors:
  • Ease of discovery: How easy to find?
  • Ease of exploit: How easy to exploit?
  • Awareness: Is it publicly known?
  • Intrusion detection: Can it be detected?

3 Estimate Impact

Evaluate technical and business impact

Technical Impact:
  • Loss of confidentiality: Data exposure severity
  • Loss of integrity: Data corruption severity
  • Loss of availability: System downtime severity
  • Loss of accountability: Audit trail damage
Business Impact:
  • Financial damage: Direct and indirect costs
  • Reputation damage: Brand and trust impact
  • Non-compliance: Regulatory penalties
  • Privacy violation: Personal data breach impact

4 Calculate Risk Severity

Combine likelihood and impact to determine overall risk

  • Formula: Risk = Likelihood √ó Impact
  • Scale: Each factor rated 0-9
  • Severity levels: Note, Low, Medium, High, Critical
  • Customize: Adjust weights based on organization

5 Prioritize & Remediate

Decide what to fix and implement solutions

  • Fix immediately: Critical and High risks
  • Schedule fix: Medium risks in next release
  • Monitor: Low risks, fix if possible
  • Accept: Note risks with documented justification

üîë OWASP Key Principles

  • Application-focused: Specifically for software security
  • Practical approach: Developer and tester friendly
  • Customizable: Tailor factors to your environment
  • Repeatable: Consistent methodology across applications
  • Top 10 integration: Works with OWASP Top 10 vulnerabilities

ISO 27005 vs OWASP Comparison

üè¢ ISO 27005

  • Scope: Enterprise-wide information security
  • Approach: Holistic risk management framework
  • Focus: All information assets and processes
  • Best for: ISMS, compliance, governance
  • Methodology: Qualitative or quantitative
  • Audience: Security managers, executives, auditors
  • Integration: ISO 27001 certification

üíª OWASP

  • Scope: Web application security
  • Approach: Specific vulnerability risk rating
  • Focus: Software vulnerabilities and exploits
  • Best for: DevSecOps, application security
  • Methodology: Structured scoring system (0-9)
  • Audience: Developers, security testers, AppSec
  • Integration: SDLC, OWASP Top 10, ASVS

üéØ When to Use Which Framework

  • Use ISO 27005 when: Implementing ISMS, seeking certification, conducting enterprise risk assessments, managing diverse asset types
  • Use OWASP when: Assessing application vulnerabilities, conducting penetration tests, prioritizing security bugs, DevSecOps integration
  • Use both together: OWASP for tactical application security + ISO 27005 for strategic enterprise risk management
  • Complementary strength: OWASP findings feed into ISO 27005 risk register
